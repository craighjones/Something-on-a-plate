Accessible nutrition

In a time where the NHS is under so much pressure from the increase of diagnosed illnesses such as obesity, heart disease, diabetes, hormonal imbalances, anxiety and depression we need to recognise that a one-size-fits-all approach is not working. Through increased availability and accessibility to information and services we can provide people with the ability to make the right decisions for their health.

Individual, group or fancy a spot of mud thrown in?

Making nutrition more accessible is what drives me as a Nutritional Therapist. Having previously worked at a number of charities and ethical businesses I’ve seen how successful behavioural change starts with education and knowledge - once someone feels they know enough about something, they feel empowered to make the right decisions. The same applies to changing attitudes towards nutrition and its impact on our health.
